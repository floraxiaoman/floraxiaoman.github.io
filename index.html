Academic Homepage

Multimodal Trustworthy AI, Deepfake Detection, and Continual Learning

I am interested in building robust multimodal systems for detecting AI-generated and manipulated media. My recent work focuses on multimodal deepfake continual detection, where detection models must adapt to emerging audio-visual forgery techniques while retaining previously learned knowledge.

This page is designed for presenting research outcomes, selected publications, open-source projects, academic service, and contact information in a GitHub Pages friendly format.

Selected papers

IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security

Adaptive Affinity Memorization with Layer Mutation for Multimodal Deepfake Continual Detection

Man Xiao, Jianbin Ye, Bo Liu, Zijian Gao, Wuyang Chen, Tao Li, Huaimin Wang, Kele Xu

Amber is a replay-based continual learning method for multimodal deepfake detection. It selects high-affinity and low-affinity memories and mutates layer-wise knowledge to preserve historical forgery knowledge while adapting to new audio-visual manipulation techniques.
